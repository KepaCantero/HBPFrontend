The Neurorobotics platform

The NeuroRobotics Platform (NRP) developed in the Human Brain Project (HBP) is an application for evaluating brain models by connecting them to physical simulations of virtual robots and environments.

From your browser!

The platform runs in a browser. That way, it is accessible to a broad audience including researchers from neuro-sciences and robotics alike. There is little to no programming involved for setting up and running experiments that are reproducible and can be easily shared among researchers. Furthermore, this approach will easily grant access to the high-performance computing clusters provided by the HBP as well as to neuromorphic hardware in the near future.

Targeted Audience

This is the first internal release of the NRP targeted at the HBP consortium. This release represents the current state of development and provides a first glance upon how the NRP will be able to contribute to active research in the Human Brain Project in the future. It marks also the first time that the platform is presented to a broader audience.

What to expect?

Please keep in mind that the NRP is currently still under active development so that the experience is limited to the execution of a single exemplary experiment that can be performed with two different, selectable robots. In this experiment, the robots act as Braitenberg vehicles, that is, their behavior is controlled by a simplistic brain consisting of only a small number of neurons that steer the robots towards red stimuli. This happens in a virtual environment with screens attached to opposite walls that can switch a displayed color upon user interaction.

This experiment, while simple, shows very well the interplay of the neural simulator and the physical robot simulations which is the core of the NRP.

In addition, one of the strongest aspects of this release lies in multi-user experiments performed on the platform. This means that the NRP offers the ability to let multiple users observe (but not interact with) experiments that have been started by other experimenters.

Altogether, this first internal release shows that the core functionality of Neurorobotics Platform has already reached an initial operational status.

Roadmap

The main focus of the ongoing and future development of the NRP will lie on the implementation of user tools for creating own experiments. In detail, this will offer experimenters the opportunity to comfortably specifying their own, more complex experiments with well-defined control flows, selecting (and, to a certain degree, creating) virtual robots and environments, and configuring the connections between brain models and the embodiments.
