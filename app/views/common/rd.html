Robot Designer

The Robot Designer development will start soon.

The Robot Designer will allow a user to easily set up a virtual robot for a given simulated experiment. The word "easily" is here very important since the user may be a neuroscientist, and unfamiliar with robotics details. The robot shall be integrated into an environment later on.

In the first version of the Robot Designer, to be completed at the end of the ramp-up phase, the Designer will provide the user with pre-assembled robots from a "robot library", or from imported files for quick customisation.

The team is currently implementing three robots for the platform: The Clearpath Husky robot, the LAURON, a bio-inspired, six-legged walking robot which was developed at the FZI in Karlsruhe as well as a biologically inspired mouse model.

The Husky is a realistic model of a mobile robot distributed by a company called Clearpath. It will therefore be a good demonstrator for the transition from simulated to physical robotics experiments, which are planned for the FPA phase of HBP.

The LAURON robot is targeted at testing and enhancing statically stable walking in difficult and rough terrain. Present work on LAURON deals with bio-inspired gait analysis, navigation strategies, robot autonomy, manipulation with its front legs, and energy-efficient walking. The mechanical design of this walking robot is biologically-inspired by the stick insect. It has 6 legs fixed to a central body with an inner aluminium skeleton, which holds the necessary electronics. Each of the 6 legs is actuated by 4 joints. Additionally, the head can be moved in 2 directions (roll, pitch) giving LAURON an overall amount of 26 degrees of freedom.

The mouse model caters to the needs of neuroscientists and the plans to run behavioural experiments in simulation.

The Neurorobotics Platform is built on top of Gazebo . Any robot created for Gazebo is therefore compatible with the platform. Gazebo uses the SDF format. If you want your robots to be available, get in contact with us! (we only need the corresponding SDF file(s) and assets).
